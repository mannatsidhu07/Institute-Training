  ### Day-12
  The objective for today was to learn and understand practically how a CRO(Cathode Ray Oscilloscope) and a function generator is used. A CRO is a powerful electronic test instrument used to visualize and analyze the waveforms of electrical signals (like voltage) over time. By graphing how a signal changes, it allows engineers and technicians to measure frequency, amplitude, and phase. 
  <br><br>
  <div align="center">
<img width="800" height="500" alt="WhatsApp Image 2026-07-19 at 14 15 26" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a1f7a671-e3f4-4e93-8c85-8cd1e81b51f3" />


  <br><br></div>
Whereas a function generator is an electronic test instrument used to generate, modify, and output various electrical waveforms. It serves as an adjustable "input" signal to test, analyze, and troubleshoot electronic circuits, allowing engineers and students to simulate how a device behaves under different real-world electrical conditions.<br><br>
